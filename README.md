## Overview

GEO Foundations is the public mathematical core of the GEO — Hidden Geometry Framework.

This repository develops a conservative geometric architecture through three coordinated modules:

* **GEO-GEOMETRY** — geometric realization of primitive conservative organization;
* **GEO-MATH** — axioms, canonical operators, harmonic organization, and invariant structures;
* **GEO-ALGEBRA** — admissible transformations, quotient structures, reconstruction, stability, and algebraic closure.

The repository is intentionally restricted to mathematical foundations.

Physical interpretations, cosmological applications, numerical laboratories, and implementation-specific studies are maintained in separate GEO repositories.

---

## Fundamental Principle

The framework is organized around the following statement:

> **Observable structures arise as projections of invariant conservative organization.**

Primitive organization precedes observation.

Observable quantities are treated as projected realizations rather than primitive mathematical objects.

## Modules

### GEO-GEOMETRY

Develops the geometric realization of the framework.

Principal topics include:

* primitive geometric organization;
* observable and complementary sectors;
* bifocal organization;
* projection geometry;
* harmonic structures;
* residual organization;
* spectral geometry;
* observable realization.

### GEO-MATH

Develops the formal mathematical architecture.

Principal topics include:

* axiomatic foundation;
* conservative decomposition;
* canonical operators;
* projective harmonic organization;
* harmonic closure;
* spectral invariants;
* observable projection.

### GEO-ALGEBRA

Formalizes the transformations admitted by the framework.

Principal topics include:

* admissible transformations;
* image/kernel decomposition;
* projective equivalence;
* quotient algebra;
* reconstruction;
* stable configurations;
* fixed-point and ring algebra;
* spectral invariance;
* algebraic closure.

## Scope

This repository establishes the mathematical basis of GEO.

It does not claim:

* experimental confirmation;
* completion of a physical theory;
* replacement of standard cosmological or gravitational models;
* validation of every application developed within the wider GEO project.

Applications must be evaluated independently from the mathematical foundation presented here.
