# GEO-MATH

# Conservative Decomposition

## Purpose

The axioms establish that primitive geometry is conservative.

The present document derives the existence of complementary sectors from that conservation principle.

No dimensional choice is introduced.

The decomposition is obtained as a structural consequence of conservation itself.

---

# Starting Point

Let

[
\mathcal G
]

be the primitive generating geometry.

By Axiom 2,

[
\mathcal C(\mathcal G)=\mathcal G.
]

The total organization is preserved.

No admissible transformation may create or destroy information.

---

# Necessity of Decomposition

Suppose an observer accesses only a subset

[
O \subset \mathcal G.
]

If

[
O=\mathcal G,
]

then observation is complete.

However, Axiom 4 states that observation is a projection.

Therefore

[
O\neq\mathcal G.
]

There must exist information not contained in the observable sector.

Hence a complementary sector necessarily exists.

Denote it by

[
B.
]

---

# Conservative Partition

Since no information may disappear,

the primitive geometry decomposes as

[
\boxed{
\mathcal G = O \oplus B.
}
]

The observable sector and the complementary sector together reconstruct the primitive organization.

Neither alone is sufficient.

---

# Uniqueness up to Equivalence

Different admissible representatives may generate the same observable description.

Therefore the decomposition is unique only up to the equivalence classes defined by admissible transformations.

This agrees with Axiom 5.

The decomposition is structural rather than coordinate dependent.

---

# Conservation Law

Because

[
\mathcal G = O \oplus B,
]

every admissible redistribution satisfies

[
O+B=\mathcal G.
]

No observable variation may alter the total organization.

Only redistribution between both sectors is permitted.

This is the mathematical origin of complementarity.

---

# Functional Complementarity

The decomposition implies that every increase in one sector must be compensated by a corresponding decrease in the other.

Symbolically,

[
\boxed{
\delta O + \delta B = 0.
}
]

Therefore complementary dynamics are tangent to the conservation manifold.

This differential relation will later generate the projective harmonic operator.

---

# Projection Interpretation

Observation acts only on

[
O.
]

The complementary sector

[
B
]

is not absent.

It is hidden by projection.

Observable incompleteness is therefore a mathematical consequence of conservative organization.

---

# Structural Consequences

The conservative decomposition establishes:

* existence of complementary structure;
* conservation of total organization;
* hidden information under projection;
* tangent redistribution between sectors.

No dimensions have yet been assigned.

No numerical constants have yet appeared.

---

# Independence from Coordinates

The decomposition

[
\mathcal G = O \oplus B
]

is invariant under admissible coordinate changes.

The choice of basis may change.

The conservative partition does not.

Thus the decomposition is an intrinsic property of the primitive geometry.

---

# Preparation for Quotient Geometry

Since observation acts only on

[
O,
]

while

[
B
]

remains hidden,

the natural mathematical object is not the full primitive geometry but its quotient by the complementary sector.

This motivates the next construction.

Conservative geometry cannot remain undivided under projection.
