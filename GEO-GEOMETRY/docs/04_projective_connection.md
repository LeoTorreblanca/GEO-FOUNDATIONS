## Purpose

The previous document established that conservative redistribution occurs along tangent directions.

It will be shown that this connection is not an additional object but the natural support through which conservative organization is transported.

The bifocal geometry contains two complementary centers

# Definition

Let

[
\Gamma
]

denote the projective connection joining the two complementary centers.

The connection is not a trajectory.

# Conservative Transport

Redistribution along

[
\Gamma
]

# Projective Character

The connection is projective.

Observable projection reveals only part of its structure.

Different projected realizations may correspond to the same underlying connection.

Consequently,

[
\Gamma
]

belongs to the primitive geometry rather than to observable space.

---

# Continuity

The projective connection is continuous.

Discontinuities would separate the complementary centers and violate conservative reconstruction.

Every admissible redistribution therefore follows a continuous projective path.

---

# Minimal Support

The connection carries no independent degrees of freedom.

Its existence is completely determined by the existence of the complementary centers.

Removing either focus eliminates the connection.

Removing the connection destroys conservative coherence.

The three objects therefore form a single inseparable organization.

---

# Information Transport

The projective connection preserves relational information.

It does not define objects.

It defines how objects remain mutually consistent.

Observable geometry is therefore constrained by the organization encoded in

[
\Gamma.
]

# Stability

The projective connection stabilizes the bifocal configuration.

Small perturbations redistribute organization along the connection rather than breaking it.
